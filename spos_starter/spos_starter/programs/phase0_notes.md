# Phase 0 Notes

Purpose:
- maximize high-quality max velocity exposure
- increase repeatable, non-forced sprinting
- identify limiting mechanism without locking in too early

Sprint control:
- controlled fast (primer reps)
- high-quality fast (main reps)
- forbidden = forced, muscular, chasing, tightening

---

## Research Synthesis — A/B/C/D

### A — Rep Structure
**Selected: Pure singles with full CNS recovery**

Options evaluated:
- Cluster sets (10–30s intra-rep rest): developed for weightlifting; insufficient recovery for max-v sprinting
- Wave loading: appropriate for lifting, not applicable to sprint exposure
- Pure singles (6–8 min full rest): supported by SimpliFaster 10m fly research — "each subsequent run began without the presence of fatigue"; maximizes quality per rep

**Why pure singles:** Max velocity can only be maintained for ~1 second. Each rep must be a fresh CNS expression. Short rests degrade quality without improving exposure. Full recovery between singles = more total high-quality reps over the session.

---

### B — Session Structure
**Selected: Constant fly distance, primer reps first**

Options evaluated:
- Ascending (increasing fly): adds complexity; not appropriate for phase where quality is the only variable
- Descending (decreasing fly): assumes fatigue accumulation as a design feature — contradicts quality-first approach
- Hybrid: introduces multiple distances per session; confounds feedback
- Constant + primers: 2 low-intensity primer reps to prime CNS, then identical working reps — simplest structure that maximizes clean exposures

**Why constant:** Research consensus (SimpliFaster, RunningMagazine) uses same fly zone repeated with full rest. No evidence that varying distances increases quality. Fixed zone allows clear detection of quality drop across reps.

---

### C — Build Distance
**Selected: 25–30m build for 15m fly**

Options evaluated:
- 20m build: common for general fitness athletes; too short for athlete breaking at 30–40m — may still be accelerating at fly entry
- 25–30m build: supported by SimpliFaster ("30m acceleration phase before 10m fly"); athlete reaches upright posture before fly zone begins
- 35m+ build: unnecessary additional distance; increases fatigue before fly

**Why 25–30m:** This athlete's max velocity breakdown occurs at 30–40m, meaning peak velocity is reached around 25–35m. A 25–30m build ensures the athlete enters the fly zone at or near max velocity — upright, not still driving. Do NOT shorten the build to squeeze in more reps.

---

### D — Phase Duration
**Selected: 3–4 weeks with quality exit criteria**

Options evaluated:
- 2 weeks: insufficient for neural adaptation (even fast neural changes require 3+ weeks of consistent stimulus)
- 3–4 weeks: appropriate for early-phase neural and coordination adaptation; supported by periodization literature (mesocycles for specific goals)
- 6–8 weeks: too long without progression; risk of accommodation

**Why 3–4 weeks:** Phase 0 targets neural stiffness and coordination — both respond within 3–4 weeks of quality exposure. Exit on criteria, not the clock.

---

## Phase 0 Program

**Phase goal:** Expose to elastic, high-quality max velocity. Improve leg stiffness, touchdown timing, and upright sprint pattern without accumulating fatigue debt.

**KPI:** Reps feel elastic and reflexive, not muscular. No quality deterioration across the session.

**Duration:** 3–4 weeks. Exit when 5+ reps are consistently elastic and GCT visibly shortens on video.

**Primary limiters targeted:** Leg stiffness at max velocity contact + touchdown timing vs COM

---

### Weekly Structure

```
Day 1 — Sprint (Max Velocity) + Lift (Heavy + Stiffness)
Day 2 — [rest or light movement]
Day 3 — Off
Day 4 — Sprint (Max Velocity) + Lift (Heavy + Stiffness)
Day 5 — Off
Day 6 — Off
Day 7 — Off
```

2 combined sprint + lift days per week. Sprint always first. Lift after.

---

### Sprint Sessions — Both Days

**Structure (both days identical):**
- Primer reps: 2 × (25–30m build → 10m fly) — relaxed effort, not counted in volume
  - Purpose: prime CNS, establish pattern, do not chase speed
  - Rest after primers: 5 min before starting main work
- Main reps: pure singles
  - 1 rep → full rest (6–8 min) → repeat
  - Build: 25–30m (bias toward longer build — athlete enters fly already upright)
  - Fly zone: 15m (constant across all reps in Phase 0)
  - Floor: 3 main reps minimum
  - Ceiling: 4–6 main reps ONLY if quality is identical to rep 1

**Stop immediately when:**
- Rep feels forced or muscular (not reflexive)
- Contact time visibly increases on video (GCT lengthening)
- Posture breaks (anterior tilt, forward lean, neck tension)

**Cues (max velocity specific):**
- "Land under you, not in front" (touchdown timing)
- "Stay tall through the fly, eyes forward" (upright posture)
- "Let the ground push you up — do not push the ground" (stiffness, not active push)

**Day 1 vs Day 2:**
- Day 1 is primary exposure
- Day 2 is confirmation — same protocol; drop to floor reps (3) if any residual fatigue from Day 1

---

### Lift Sessions

#### Day 1 — Heavy Squat + Stiffness

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Back squat | 3–5 × 3–5 | Heavy, stop 1–2 reps before grind. No grinding. |
| RDL | 3 × 5–6 | Full hip hinge, controlled descent |
| Single-leg drop landing → hold | 3 × 4–5/leg | Step off box ~30cm, land stiff, hold 2s. Fast, not heavy. Touchdown coordination. |
| Ankle-stiff pogos | 2–3 × 6 | GCT < 150ms. Reflexive, not jumpy. Optional if Day 1 sprint leaves capacity. |

#### Day 2 — Heavy Squat + Strength

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Back squat | 3–5 × 3–5 | Heavy, stop 1–2 reps before grind |
| RDL | 3 × 5–6 | Full hip hinge, controlled descent |
| Leg curl or Nordic curl | 3 × 4–5 | Controlled but aggressive contraction. Supports RFD and hip extension under high velocity. |
| Copenhagen hold | 2 × 10s/side | Optional. Adductor stability. Keep if athlete shows medial weakness. |

**Accessory rules:**
- Must map directly to sprint execution (stiffness, touchdown, hip extension)
- No band work, no low-stimulus movements
- Max 2 accessories per day. 1 is preferred Day 2.

---

### Diagnostics

**Coordination issue (Mechanism #2 primary):**
- Every rep feels like "reaching" — extending foot forward to find ground
- Foot landing visibly in front of hip on video
- Braking sensation at contact
- Action: reduce fly to 10m, focus on touchdown cues. Add primer drill: A-skip → immediate fly entry.

**Force expression issue (Mechanism #1 primary):**
- Reps feel effortful/muscular — grinding, not bouncing
- Visible GCT lengthening on video (hip drops at contact)
- No spring quality across session
- Action: reduce volume to floor (3 reps), add drop landing emphasis in lift

**Exposure issue (insufficient stimulus):**
- Quality holds but no improvement across weeks
- Reps feel easy but no elasticity development
- Action: increase fly to 20m, extend build by 5m to increase entry velocity

---

### Progression Rules

**Add reps:**
When ceiling reps feel as clean as rep 1 across 2 consecutive sessions → add 1 rep next session (max 6 main reps per session)

**Extend fly distance:**
When 5+ main reps are elastic at 15m fly (2 consecutive sessions) → extend fly to 20m. Hold at 20m until quality confirms. Then → 25m.

**Reduce:**
Any forced rep on Day 1 → drop to floor reps only (3 reps), hold fly distance. Do not reduce build distance.

**Phase exit criteria:**
- 5+ main reps consistently elastic (athlete reports reflexive feel, not muscular)
- GCT visibly shorter on video (hip position higher at contact)
- Quality holds across both days without fatigue drop
- Phase exit: 3 weeks minimum, 4 weeks typical. Exit on criteria, not clock.
